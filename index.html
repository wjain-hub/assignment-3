Dieter Rams

10 Principles of Good Design

Read More

Dieter Rams, born in Wiesbaden, Germany, in 1932, was deeply influenced by his grandfather, a carpenter. Initially trained as an architect, Rams transitioned into industrial design when he joined Braun in 1955. Under the mentorship of Ulm School of Design pioneers, he quickly rose to Head of Design (1961-1995), shaping the company's legacy with groundbreaking, minimalist  products.

In 1957, Rams began designing furniture for Vitsœ, leading to the creation of the 606 Universal Shelving System—a design still in production today. His dual career at Braun and Vitsœ cemented his reputation as one of the most influential designers of the 20th century.

Rams believed in design as responsibility. In a 1976 speech, he warned against wastefulness and the thoughtless consumption of resources. His philosophy—centered on simplicity, functionality, and longevity—gave rise to his famous "Ten Principles of Good Design." Even today, his work and ideals continue to inspire designers worldwide.

Rams' contributions have been exhibited globally, and he, along with his wife, established the Dieter and Ingeborg Rams Foundation to promote responsible design. His 1971 home remains preserved, reflecting his vision of timeless, meaningful living.
